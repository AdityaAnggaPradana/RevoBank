Project Summary

This project revolves around analyzing the performance of RevoBank's promotional program in collaboration with RevoShop, an e-commerce marketplace. The aim is to comprehend customer spending patterns, categorize customers based on their responsiveness to promotions, and customize the rewards program for various customer segments

Project Background


RevoBank, a European bank, collaborates with RevoShop, an e-commerce marketplace, for a promotion targeting credit card users. RevoBank offers vouchers to customers, which can be redeemed for credit card reward points. After running this for six months, they want to understand customer spending patterns, identify different user segments, and customize the rewards program more effectively. This is to make the promotion work better for both customers and RevoBank.

Goals


The business requirement/goal of the analysis is to assess the performance of RevoBank's promotion program with RevoShop and identify opportunities to customize the rewards program for different segments of RevoBank credit card users who are also RevoShop customers.

Objectives

  • Performance Overview: Provide insights into how the promotion program has performed from RevoBank's perspective over the past 6 months. This includes understanding the impact of the promotion on credit card usage, transaction volume, revenue generated, and associated costs.
  • Customer Segmentation: Identify different segments of RevoBank credit card users who are also RevoShop customers. The goal is to group customers based on their spending patterns, behavior, and responsiveness to the promotion.

    Summary Clustering Analysis


    Interpretation:
    1. Cluster 0 : Customers in this group are high spenders but do not make a lot of transactions. They are highly responsive to promotions, meaning offering them targeted promotions could encourage more spending.
    2. Cluster 1 : Customers in this group also spend a lot but make slightly more transactions compared to Cluster 0. They are not as sensitive to promotions as Cluster 0, so offering promotions may not have a significant impact on their spending.
    3. Cluster 2 : Customers in this group make many transactions but have a lower average spending amount. They are sensitive to promotions, indicating that offering them targeted promotions could lead to increased spending.

    Recommendation

    1. Cluster 0: Customize promotions for this segment to encourage more spending, as they are highly responsive to promotions. This can lead to increased revenue with a relatively low promotional cost.
    2. Cluster 1: Instead of relying solely on promotions, explore other marketing strategies or introduce rewards for this segment. Promotions may not significantly impact their spending behavior, so diversifying your approach is advisable.
    3. Cluster 2: Offer targeted promotions to customers in this segment to stimulate their frequent transactions and boost overall spending. They are sensitive to promotions and can be encouraged to spend more through this approach.

    In summary, focus on Cluster 0 and Cluster 2 for promotions, as they are more likely to respond positively and generate higher revenue with relatively low promotional costs. For Cluster 1, consider alternative strategies as promotions may not have a substantial impact on their spending behavior
